Account Types

We offer several types of foreign currency accounts to meet your specific needs:

Foreign Currency Savings Account

Earn interest on your foreign currency deposits while maintaining full access to your funds.

  • Competitive interest rates on major currencies
  • Access to online and mobile banking
  • Monthly statements in electronic or paper format
  • Minimum balance requirement varies by currency
  • International fund transfers and remittances

Foreign Currency Current Account

Designed for frequent transactions, ideal for businesses and individuals who need to make regular international payments.

  • Checkbook facility (available for select currencies)
  • International debit card (upon request)
  • No limit on number of transactions
  • Access to our global banking network
  • Business banking support for international trade

Foreign Currency Fixed Deposit

Lock in attractive interest rates for a fixed term while holding your deposits in foreign currency.

  • Higher interest rates than savings accounts
  • Terms ranging from 1 month to 5 years
  • Option to auto-renew upon maturity
  • Interest can be paid in any of our supported currencies
  • Early withdrawal available (subject to terms and conditions)

Multi-Currency Account

A single account that allows you to hold and manage multiple currencies simultaneously.

  • Hold up to 10 different currencies in one account
  • Seamless currency conversion between account balances
  • Single statement showing all currency balances
  • Consolidated view in online and mobile banking
  • Preferential exchange rates for inter-account transfers

How It Works

Opening and managing a foreign currency account with Bangko Filipino is simple and straightforward:

1

Apply for an Account

Visit any of our branches with valid identification, proof of address, and initial deposit (minimum amount varies by currency). Existing customers can also apply through our online banking platform or mobile app.

2

Account Setup

Once your application is approved, we'll set up your account and provide you with all necessary details for managing your foreign currency funds. This typically takes 1-2 business days.

3

Fund Your Account

Deposit funds into your account through international wire transfer, currency exchange from your peso account, or direct deposit of foreign currency checks or cash at our branches.

4

Manage Your Account

Access and manage your foreign currency account through our secure online banking platform, mobile app, or visit any of our branches. Monitor your balance, make transfers, and exchange currencies as needed.

Frequently Asked Questions

Who can open a foreign currency account?
Our foreign currency accounts are available to Filipino citizens, residents, non-resident Filipinos, foreign nationals with proper identification, and registered businesses. Documentation requirements may vary depending on your residency status and the type of account you wish to open.
What is the minimum balance requirement?
Minimum balance requirements vary by currency and account type. For example, our standard USD savings account requires a minimum balance of USD 500, while GBP accounts require a minimum of GBP 300. Please contact our customer service or visit a branch for specific requirements for other currencies.
Can I exchange between different currencies within my account?
Yes, our multi-currency account allows for seamless exchange between currencies. For separate foreign currency accounts, you can transfer funds between accounts and convert currencies at preferential exchange rates. These transactions can be performed through our online banking platform, mobile app, or at any branch.
Are foreign currency accounts insured?
Yes, deposits in foreign currency accounts are insured by the Philippine Deposit Insurance Corporation (PDIC) up to the equivalent of PHP 500,000 per depositor. This insurance applies to the peso equivalent of your foreign currency deposits at the time of bank closure, if such an event were to occur.
Can I receive my salary or other regular payments in my foreign currency account?
Absolutely. You can provide your foreign currency account details to your employer or payers for direct deposit. We provide complete account information including SWIFT codes and correspondent bank details to facilitate smooth international transfers.
What documents do I need to open a foreign currency account?
Generally, you'll need valid government-issued photo identification, proof of address (utility bill, etc.), tax identification number, and source of funds documentation. Business accounts require additional documentation such as business registration certificates and board resolutions. Non-residents may need to provide additional proof of identity and address verification.
