Trade Finance Overview

Trade finance represents a crucial aspect of international commerce, providing businesses with the financial tools and security mechanisms needed to engage in global trade. At Bangko Filipino, we understand the complexities of international transactions and offer tailored trade finance solutions to mitigate risks, enhance cash flow, and facilitate seamless trade operations.

Our trade finance services bridge the gap between exporters who want guaranteed payment and importers who want to ensure goods are received before payment is released. With our extensive global network and expert team, we provide end-to-end support for your cross-border trading activities.

Our Trade Finance Services

Letters of Credit

Secure payment mechanism where we commit to pay the exporter on behalf of the importer once shipping documents are presented that comply with the terms and conditions of the LC.

Bank Guarantees

Financial backing that ensures contractual obligations are met, providing security to beneficiaries that payment will be made if specified conditions are not fulfilled.

Documentary Collections

Facilitation of document exchange between parties where we act as an intermediary to collect payment from the buyer in exchange for shipping documents.

Export Financing

Pre and post-shipment financing options to help exporters maintain healthy cash flow while waiting for payment from international buyers.

Import Financing

Credit facilities that allow importers to pay suppliers upfront while deferring actual payment to the bank until goods are received and sold.

Trade Risk Mitigation

Solutions to protect against non-payment, currency fluctuations, political risks, and other uncertainties in international trade.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is a Letter of Credit and how does it work?

A Letter of Credit (LC) is a payment mechanism used in international trade where a bank guarantees that a buyer's payment to a seller will be received on time and for the correct amount. If the buyer is unable to make payment on the purchase, the bank will cover the full or remaining amount of the purchase.

The basic workflow involves: the importer applies for an LC, the issuing bank issues the LC, the advising bank verifies and notifies the exporter, the exporter ships goods and submits documents, and finally, payment is made once all conditions are met.

How can trade finance help my business?

Trade finance can benefit your business in numerous ways, including:

  • Reducing payment risks in international transactions
  • Providing access to working capital during the trade cycle
  • Bridging the time gap between export and payment receipt
  • Enabling you to offer competitive payment terms to buyers
  • Protecting against currency fluctuations and political risks
  • Facilitating entry into new international markets
What's the difference between a bank guarantee and a standby letter of credit?

While both instruments serve as payment security, there are subtle differences:

Bank Guarantees are typically used to guarantee performance or financial obligations and are governed by local laws. They're commonly used for construction projects, bid bonds, or advance payment guarantees.

Standby Letters of Credit serve as a "backup" payment method and are governed by international rules (typically ISP98 or UCP 600). They're commonly used in international trade as a fallback if the primary payment method fails.

Both instruments are only triggered when a party fails to fulfill their contractual obligations.

What documentation is required for trade finance facilities?

Documentation requirements vary depending on the specific trade finance instrument, but commonly include:

  • Financial statements and business registration documents
  • Trade contracts or purchase orders
  • Import/export licenses
  • Shipping documents (bills of lading, airway bills)
  • Commercial invoices and packing lists
  • Certificates of origin
  • Insurance certificates
  • Inspection certificates

Our trade finance specialists will guide you through the specific documentation needed for your transaction.

How long does it take to set up trade finance facilities?

The timeline varies based on the complexity of the transaction and the type of facility required. Generally:

  • For new clients, setting up initial trade finance facilities may take 2-4 weeks, including due diligence and credit approval.
  • For existing clients with established facilities, individual transactions like issuing a Letter of Credit can be completed within 1-3 business days once all documentation is correctly submitted.
  • Documentary collections typically take 1-2 business days to process.

Our team works efficiently to minimize processing times while ensuring thorough risk assessment.
