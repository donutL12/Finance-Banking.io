Frequently Asked Questions

What documents do I need to open a foreign currency account?
To open a foreign currency account with Bangko Filipino, you'll need to provide your valid ID, proof of address, tax identification number, and depending on the purpose of the account, additional documentation such as business registration certificates for business accounts or work permits for expatriates. Our international banking specialists can guide you through the specific requirements based on your situation.
How long does an international wire transfer take?
Most international wire transfers are completed within 1-3 business days, depending on the destination country, recipient bank, and the time of day the transfer is initiated. Transfers to countries with established banking relationships may be faster. You can track the status of your transfer through our online banking platform or mobile app.
Are there minimum balance requirements for foreign currency accounts?
Yes, foreign currency accounts typically have minimum balance requirements that vary by currency type. For major currencies like USD, EUR, or JPY, the minimum balance requirement is lower compared to exotic currencies. Please contact our customer service or visit your nearest branch for specific minimum balance requirements for the currency of your interest.
What is a letter of credit and how does it work?
A letter of credit (LC) is a document issued by a bank guaranteeing that a seller will receive payment as long as certain delivery conditions are met. In international trade, it reduces the risk for both buyers and sellers. Bangko Filipino can issue LCs on behalf of importers and advise or confirm LCs received by exporters, ensuring secure international transactions.
