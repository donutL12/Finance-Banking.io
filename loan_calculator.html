Understanding Your Loan

Making informed decisions about loans requires understanding key terms and factors that affect your finances. Here's what you need to know:

Principal Amount

This is the original sum of money borrowed, excluding interest charges. The higher the principal, the higher your monthly payments will be.

Interest Rate

The percentage charged on the loan amount, representing the cost of borrowing money. Even a small difference in interest rate can significantly impact your total payments.

Loan Term

The period over which you'll repay the loan. Longer terms result in lower monthly payments but higher total interest paid over time.

Amortization

The process of gradually paying off a loan with regular payments. In the early years, a larger portion of each payment goes toward interest rather than principal.
