Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about our investment accounts and services.

What is the minimum investment amount required? ‚ñº

The minimum investment amount varies depending on the investment product. For mutual funds, the minimum is typically PHP 5,000. For direct stock investments, we recommend starting with at least PHP 50,000 to build a diversified portfolio.

What investment products does Bangko Filipino offer? ‚ñº

We offer a wide range of investment products including mutual funds, stocks, bonds, ETFs (Exchange-Traded Funds), UITFs (Unit Investment Trust Funds), government securities, and alternative investments. Our investment advisors can help you select the right mix based on your goals and risk tolerance.

How are my investments protected? ‚ñº

Your investments with Bangko Filipino are protected through several mechanisms. We are regulated by the Securities and Exchange Commission (SEC) and the Bangko Sentral ng Pilipinas (BSP). Additionally, we maintain segregated client accounts, meaning your investments are held separately from the bank's assets.

Can I access my investment account online? ‚ñº

Yes, once your investment account is set up, you'll have 24/7 access to your portfolio through our secure online banking platform. You can view your portfolio performance, make additional investments, and adjust your investment strategy as needed.

What fees are associated with investment accounts? ‚ñº

Fee structures vary by investment product. For mutual funds and UITFs, there may be sales loads (entry/exit fees) and annual management fees. For stock trading, there are broker commissions. A full fee schedule will be provided during your consultation with our investment advisor.

How quickly can I liquidate my investments if needed? ‚ñº

Liquidity varies by investment type. Stocks and ETFs typically settle within 3 business days. Mutual funds and UITFs may take 3-7 business days. Some fixed-income securities and alternative investments may have longer liquidation periods. Your investment advisor will explain the liquidity considerations for each product.
